What data is used to generate similarity statistics of my account?

Similarity statistics is aggregated on the level of entire Unicheck account. It means Unicheck statistics is based on data collected from similarity checks done under this account in both, Unicheck Integrations and Standalone version. Statistics is not generated for a certain part of data, like one Integration, a course or Instructor account in Standalone.

Where can I view similarity statistics?

Similarity statistics is available for view in Unicheck Administrator account and Unicheck subordinated Instructor accounts in Standalone.

  1. To see statistics log in to your account at corp.unicheck.com.
  2. In your Unicheck account click on your avatar in the top right corner next to your name.
  3. Choose Statistics in the drop-down menu.

How many documents have been checked for similarity by our Institution?

In Overview section look at the number of documents that have been checked for similarity since Administrator account activation. Renewal of your licence plan does not influence this calculator.

How many Students of our Institution use Unicheck service under our licence?

In Overview section check the number of Active Students - users who submitted at least one document for similarity check in the role of a Student via any Unicheck Integration and/or Unicheck Standalone version. If you want to check your current number of unique active students according to FTE licence, refer to this number.

Note: In case of renewal of your licence, this counter will be restarted.

How many Instructors of our Institution have Unicheck accounts?

In Overview section check the number of Unicheck Instructor accounts created by Administrator at corp.unicheck.com. This number is available only if you use Standalone version, because Integrated to LMS Unicheck app version does not require the creation of Unicheck Instructor accounts.

What share of documents submitted for similarity check in our Institution is original?

  1. In Similarity vs Originality section choose the date range to see the share of original documents for a certain period of time.
  2. View green color sector and percentage to find out original documents share for a set time period. Original are considered to be submissions with similarity score less than 5%.

Blue, orange and red shows a share of documents with matches from Web, Library, Library and Web respectively. Under the pie chart see the number of documents checked for similarity within the period of time you have set in action 1.

What is an average similarity rate of all submissions checked in our Institution?

  1. In Similarity vs Originality section choose the date range to see the average similarity rate for a certain period of time.
  2. Scroll to see aggregate percentage of average similarity rate for the period of time youâ€™ve set. Below the percentage there is distribution of documents by similarity rate that were checked within same period of time.

How does similarity rate of our Students submissions change over time?

In Dynamics section choose the date range to see the average similarity rate for a certain period of time. Select to see percentage calculated for every day, week or month.

Using same logic modify the second chart to see how original documents share of Students submissions changes over time.

What web sources are the most popular origin of matches?

In Popular sources section find the rate of links. Top number of matches detected in submissions are from these sources.

To see the full list, click Show more button down the page.
